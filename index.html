Dear Friends, Partners, and Community Members,

It is with a heart full of gratitude that I welcome you to the March 2026 edition of The Healing Hands. COVER STORY

Bringing Healthcare Closer: Strengthening Outreach Services in Our Catchment Area

Zimba Mission Hospital continues to extend essential healthcare services beyond the hospital through routine outreach activities conducted in surrounding communities. These outreach visits are designed to improve access to care for populations that face challenges reaching the facility, particularly mothers, children, and patients requiring ongoing follow-up.

During the recent outreach activities, health teams consisting of nurses, clinical officers, and community health assistants provided integrated services including antenatal care, under-five clinics, immunization, health education, and screening for common conditions such as hypertension and malaria. These services are often delivered in collaboration with community health workers and local leadership to ensure effective mobilization and continuity of care.

“Outreach services are a vital part of our work because they allow us to reach people early and provide preventive care,” noted a member of the clinical team. “We are able to identify health concerns, support mothers during pregnancy, and ensure children receive essential immunizations.”

MATERNAL HEALTH

Safe Births Initiative: 34% Reduction in Maternal Mortality

Zimba Mission Hospital continues to improve maternal health through skilled birth attendance and midwife training, achieving a 34% reduction in maternal mortality in our catchment area. Challenges such as long distances and late antenatal bookings remain, but success stories like Grace, a mother of twins who delivered safely, show the impact of quality, compassionate care.

HOSPITAL SAFETY & INFRASTRUCTURE

New Macroburn Installed

Zimba Mission Hospital continues to advance patient safety and quality care. We are proud to announce the successful installation of a Macroburn medical incinerator, a modern system for safe and efficient disposal of hazardous healthcare waste. Operating at temperatures of 800–1200°C, it ensures complete destruction of infectious materials such as used syringes, contaminated dressings, and other clinical waste. This installation strengthens infection prevention and control, reduces risk of disease transmission, and supports compliance with environmental and public health standards. It also promotes cleaner, more sustainable healthcare practices across our facility. We thank our dedicated staff, partners, and supporters who make milestones like this possible — together, we are building a safer, healthier future for Zimba and our community.

PATIENT SPOTLIGHT

Caring for Our Most Vulnerable: Meet Samuel

Samuel, a young patient in our care, is oxygen-dependent due to a chronic respiratory condition. Despite his daily health challenges, the dedicated staff at Zimba Mission Hospital provide round-the-clock support, ensuring he receives oxygen therapy, monitoring, and compassionate care.

His journey highlights the importance of specialized care, patient-centered support, and the tireless commitment of our medical team in improving quality of life for those with chronic conditions. Samuel’s resilience, combined with the hospital’s care, inspires hope for families facing similar health challenges in our community.

HOSPITAL INFRASTRUCTURE UPDATE

Addressing Facility Challenges to Improve Patient Care

Zimba Mission Hospital continues to provide essential health services to the community despite ongoing infrastructure challenges. Our outpatient department (OPD) building, which serves hundreds of patients daily, is currently experiencing roof leaks during the rainy season. This affects the comfort and safety of patients and presents challenges for staff in delivering consistent care.

In addition, frequent load shedding from ZESCO disrupts critical operations, including laboratory work and refrigeration of essential medicines and vaccines. To address this sustainably, the hospital is exploring the installation of solar power systems, which would ensure reliable electricity without the high cost of fuel.

We are actively seeking support from partners, donors, and well-wishers to help fund these solar installations and critical repairs. Your contribution will directly improve patient care, protect vital medical supplies, and enhance the working environment for our dedicated healthcare staff.
